# Youth Basketball Plyometrics & Speed Regimen (Ages 13-14)

## 📌 Phase Overview
*   **Objective:** Maximize explosive burst, vertical jump, and injury resilience.
*   **Safety Rule:** Quality over quantity. Every jump must be 100% maximal effort with perfect landing mechanics (soft knees, quiet feet, hips back).
*   **Equipment:** Dumbbells (DB), improvised stable box/porch step (12–18 inches).

---

## 📅 Week 1: Transition Week (No Camp)
*Goal: Build a baseline, introduce single-leg power, and prepare the nervous system.*

### Wednesday (Day 1)
*   **Warm-Up**  (4 Minutes)
*Perform each exercise continuously for **30 seconds**.*
    *   Jog in place
    *   High knees
    *   Butt kicks
    *   Jumping jacks
    *   Leg swings
    *   Arm circles
    *   Bodyweight squats
    *   Light skips
*   **Main Workout**  (14 Minutes)
*Complete **2 rounds** total. Follow a structural pattern of **40 seconds of work** followed by **20 seconds of rest** for each movement.*
    *   **Squat Jumps**
    *   **Lateral Line Hops**
    *   **Skater Jumps**
    *   **Tuck Jumps**
    *   **Broad Jumps** *(with a light walk back to start)*
    *   **Split Jump Lunges**
    *   **Fast Mountain Climbers**
*   **Cool-Down** (2 Minutes)
*Hold each static stretch for **20 to 30 seconds** to lower your heart rate.*
    *   Hamstring stretch
    *   Quad stretch
    *   Calf stretch
    *   Hip flexor stretch

### Thursday - Active Recovery & Core
*Focus on flushing out soreness from Wednesday and building core stability.*
*   **Warm-Up:** Use the standard 4-minute dynamic warm-up.
*   **Main Core Circuit (3 Rounds - 45s work, 15s rest):**
    *   Plank holds
    *   Bird-dogs (slow and controlled)
    *   Glute bridges (squeeze at top for 2 seconds)
    *   Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts (Light weight, focus on hip hinge)
*   **Cool-Down:** Standard 2-minute stretch routine.

### Friday - Explosive Power & Velocity
*Focus on maximum horizontal power and deceleration.*
*   **Warm-Up:** Standard 4-minute dynamic warm-up.
*   **Main Workout (2 Rounds - 45s work, 15s rest):**
    *   Broad Jumps (Stick the landing for 2 seconds before walking back)
    *   Skater Jumps (Focus on lateral distance)
    *   Pogo Hops (Ankle-only bouncing, minimal knee bend for tendon stiffness)
    *   Dumbbell Goblet Squats (Explosive up, 3 seconds down)
    *   Single-Leg Line Hops (Forward/Backward - 20s left leg, 20s right leg)
*   **Cool-Down:** Standard 2-minute stretch routine.

### Saturday - Vertical Peak Day
*Focus on maximum vertical displacement using the improvised box.*
*   **Warm-Up:** Standard 4-minute dynamic warm-up.
*   **Main Workout (2 Rounds - 40s work, 20s rest):**
    *   Box Jumps (Step down carefully, never jump backward off the box)
    *   Tuck Jumps (Maximum height)
    *   Split Jump Lunges 
    *   Dumbbell Thrusters (Light dumbbells, explosive overhead press)
    *   Lateral Line Hops (Side to side, max speed)
*   **Cool-Down:** Standard 2-minute stretch routine.

### Sunday - Rest & Reset
*   **Activity:** Complete rest. No structural exercise. Zero impact. 

---

## 📅 Week 2: Basketball Camp Supplement Week
*Goal: Maintain power without overloading joints. Workouts are brief (10 mins) and completed **before** camp as a primer, or 4+ hours **after** camp concludes.*

### Monday - Pre-Camp CNS Primer
*Do this right before camp to activate the nervous system for explosive speed.*
*   **Warm-Up:** Standard 4-minute dynamic warm-up.
*   **Main Primer (1 Round Only - Max Effort):**
    *   30s Pogo Hops (Fast ground contact)
    *   30s Lateral Line Hops
    *   3 Max-Effort Broad Jumps (Full rest between jumps)
*   **Cool-Down:** Handled by the camp's natural progression.

### Tuesday - Post-Camp Strength & Armor
*No jumping today. Focus on protecting the knees and ankles from camp fatigue.*
*   **Timing:** At least 2 hours after camp ends.
*   **Main Workout (2 Rounds - 10-12 repetitions each):**
    *   Dumbbell Goblet Squats (Slow, deep, controlled)
    *   Calf Raises (Holding dumbbells, 3-second hold at the top)
    *   Plank Rows (Renegade rows with light dumbbells)
    *   Single-Leg Glute Bridges
*   **Cool-Down:** Comprehensive 5-minute lower body stretching.

### Wednesday - Mid-Week Unloading (Rest)
*   **Activity:** No supplemental workouts. The 4-hour camp session is the entire workload. Focus heavily on hydration and sleep.

### Thursday - Power & Deceleration
*Maintaining vertical pop while tired.*
*   **Timing:** Evening, well after camp.
*   **Main Workout (2 Rounds - 30s work, 30s rest for high quality):**
    *   Box Jumps (Focus on explosive hip extension)
    *   Skater Jumps (Focus on stopping/sticking the landing cleanly)
    *   Fast Mountain Climbers
*   **Cool-Down:** Standard 2-minute stretch routine.

### Friday - Camp Wrap-Up Rest
*   **Activity:** No supplemental workouts. Allow them to finish the final day of camp safely.

### Saturday - Performance Test Day
*Re-introduce the baseline workout to see how they perform after camp week.*
*   **Warm-Up:** Standard 4-minute dynamic warm-up.
*   **Main Workout:** Run the exact **Day 1 Baseline Workout**.
*   **Cool-Down:** Standard 2-minute stretch routine.

### Sunday - Rest & Recovery
*   **Activity:** Complete rest.
